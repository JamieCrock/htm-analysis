Expert Assessment — The Five Headline Differences

  • Governance architecture: HTM 03-01 mandates a Ventilation Safety Group (VSG), Authorised Engineer (Ventilation) [AE(V)] and independent Validator. None existed in HTM 2025, fundamentally restructuring accountability on every project.
  • Infection control: HTM 03-01 was reviewed against COVID-19 airborne transmission evidence and 27 years of HCAI research — providing substantially enhanced guidance for isolation, transplant and immunocompromised patient areas not addressed in HTM 2025.
  • Energy regulation: HTM 03-01 mandates EU ErP Directive 1253/2014 compliance and requires electronically commutated (EC) fans as the default — a requirement entirely absent from HTM 2025.
  • Derogation accountability: Any departure from HTM 03-01 must be formally risk-assessed, authorised in writing by the Senior Responsible Owner and supported by the VSG. HTM 2025 contained no equivalent process.
  • Standards alignment: HTM 03-01 references BS EN 1822 (H13/H14), BS EN 1886 AHU airtightness and BESA DW143 duct leakage — replacing the outdated EU Grade filter system of HTM 2025.

Energy, Environment & Sustainability

HTM 03-01 was developed within the NHS Net Zero Carbon strategy and EU ErP Directive context — areas entirely absent from HTM 2025.

New
2025No sustainability or carbon reduction requirements. Energy noted only in context of annual consumption calculations.

Key Findings & Practitioner Actions

The most consequential divergences and the actions required when moving from HTM 2025 to HTM 03-01 compliance.

Critical Actions for Practitioners Moving to HTM 03-01

  • Establish a Ventilation Safety Group (VSG) before any design work begins. This mandatory governance body must formally approve derogations and sign off major design decisions in writing.
  • Appoint an Authorised Engineer (Ventilation) [AE(V)] and an independent Validator. Both are new mandatory roles — their absence will block formal project handover and sign-off.
  • Re-specify all filter classifications from EU Grade numbering (1–9) to BS EN 1822 (EPA10, H13, H14). This affects all procurement schedules, O&M manuals and acceptance test criteria.
  • Remove ceiling void AHUs and FCUs from clinical space designs. HTM 03-01 explicitly prohibits this. Refurbishment projects must relocate existing units or obtain formal written VSG dispensation.
  • Make energy recovery mandatory on all supply/extract systems — plate heat exchanger, run-around coil or thermal wheel. Omission requires regulatory justification under EU ErP 1253/2014.
  • Switch to EC fans as the default technology rather than belt-drive centrifugal units — consistent with EU ErP Directive compliance and the HTM 03-01 recommendation.
  • Upgrade UCV acceptance testing from velocity measurement to full particle penetration challenge tests: ≤10% outer zone, ≤1% inner zone, ≤0.1% centre. DOP test access must be maintained.
  • Formalise all derogation documentation. Any departure requires written risk assessment, SRO authorisation and VSG support — with a schedule of derogations for externally approved business cases.
  • Apply the natural ventilation hierarchy to non-clinical areas. Start from natural/mixed-mode; specify mechanical only when the first two options are proven inadequate.
  • Create and maintain a ventilation system inventory using the HTM 03-01 standard labelling methodology — now a mandatory project deliverable.

Overall Expert Conclusion

HTM 03-01 represents a comprehensive modernisation rather than an incremental update to HTM 2025. The most consequential shifts are in governance architecture (VSG, AE(V), independent validation), infection control scope (driven by 27 years of HCAI research and COVID-19 airborne transmission review), energy regulation compliance (EC fans, mandatory heat recovery aligned to EU ErP Directive 1253/2014), and adoption of current European test standards (BS EN 1822 filtration, BS EN 1886 AHU airtightness, BESA DW143 duct leakage testing).

Facilities operating under HTM 2025 should treat migration to HTM 03-01 as a substantive compliance gap exercise, not a routine update. Any NHS capital project entering the approval process from PID or SOC stage onwards must demonstrate full HTM 03-01 compliance as the baseline — with all derogations formally documented and VSG-approved.
